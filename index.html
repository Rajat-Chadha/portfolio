Project 01 — AFOSR

Configurable Robotic Arm on Air-Bearing Spacecraft Simulator

Designing, constructing, programming, and operating a configurable robotic arm mechanism with variable static parameters — including link mass, length, and inertia — mounted on an air-bearing spacecraft simulator. The goal is to study the dynamic coupling effect in space manipulator systems (SMS): how motion of a robotic arm affects the attitude and trajectory of its host spacecraft in a near-frictionless environment. This testbed directly supports the AFOSR-funded project on understanding and addressing dynamic coupling for challenging in-space operations.

Space Robotics Dynamic Coupling Air-Bearing Testbed ROS2 Spacecraft Simulator AFOSR

Project 02 — IASRL

Zero-Speed Crossing Effect & Peak Current Dynamics in a Reaction Wheel

Investigating the Zero-Speed Crossing Effect and peak current dynamics in a reaction wheel using a 3-DOF air-bearing testbed. Reaction wheels are critical attitude control actuators on spacecraft, but they exhibit nonlinear behavior at zero-speed crossings — a transition that can cause torque disturbances and instability. This work characterizes these dynamics experimentally, supporting the development of more robust attitude control strategies for small satellites and spacecraft with momentum exchange devices.
